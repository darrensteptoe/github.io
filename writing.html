    Institutional Clarity Series

    A nonfiction series about how institutions shape what we believe, what we reward, and what we call “truth.” The goal is practical clarity: how to think cleanly, communicate plainly, and design systems that don’t quietly punish good intentions.

    Book 1 — What Is Reliable Knowledge?

    A framework for deciding what to trust — and why — across media, politics, institutions, and everyday life.

    Book 2 — When Good Intentions Fail

    How incentives, friction, and organizational structure can overwhelm moral intent — and what to do about it.

    Book 3 — What Design Decides

    How design choices (in policy, software, organizations, and public systems) turn values into outcomes.
